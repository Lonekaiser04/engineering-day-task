Celebrating innovation & Creativity

Engineer's Day is celebrated every year on 15th September in India to honor the birth Anniversary of Bharat Ratna Mokshagundam Visvervaraya , one of the greatest engineers of Inida. This day recognizes the contribution of engineers in building our nation and shaping the future.

Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya was born on September 15, 1861, in Muddenahalli, Karnataka. He was a visionary engineer, scholar, and statesman who made significant contributions to the field of engineering and played a crucial role in the development of modern India. He is best known for his work in irrigation and water supply systems, as well as his innovative designs for dams and flood control measures. Visvesvaraya's most notable achievement was the design and construction of the Krishna Raja Sagara Dam in Mysore, which remains one of the largest reservoirs in India. He was also instrumental in the establishment of several educational institutions, including the University of Mysore and the Indian Institute of Science. In recognition of his contributions to engineering and nation-building, he was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, in 1955. Engineer's Day serves as a reminder of his legacy and inspires future generations to pursue excellence in engineering and contribute to the progress of society.

Engineers are the architects of the future, shaping the world with innovation and creativity.
